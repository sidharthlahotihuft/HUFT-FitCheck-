Subject: Pet apparel sizing gap — where I differ, and how data gets us there

Hi team,

Agreed, with two small differences. First, when I said the charts aren't "wrong" — I mean they're not the problem, just not calibrated to local sizes yet. A local 18 landing as our 20, 22, even 3XL isn't a wrong measurement, it's two scales that were never mapped to each other. Second, data alone won't solve this — it shows us the gap, not how to close it. That still needs market judgment and retail experience, which is why the PawSuite feedback loop matters as much as the data.

To start, I've already mapped the research data we have into a chart. Now we need another layer to verify it — and that layer is data.

The starting point should be our own sales behaviour, layer by layer: customer bought apparel, customer returned it, customer rebought or didn't. Each line is an insight — return/exchange rates by size, city and breed, repeat-purchase drift, cart abandonment on sizing. That's the diagnosis; the chart mapping is the fix.

This isn't theoretical. Our Indian-Readiness dataset shows how thin the ground truth is — only a few breeds have real Indian measurements, the rest are Western-chart estimates, and every time a real measurement replaced one, the estimate was wrong. That's proof we need more sizing study for India, and any new study should start from this dataset, not from scratch.

To sharpen it further, we should add: fit-lab measurements, return/exchange data, local market/tailor conventions, 3P apparel size charts as a benchmark, customer self-reported sizes at checkout, and the PawSuite retail feedback loop — our store staff fitting dogs in person is our best ground truth.

This isn't apparel-only — it should be a shared source of truth with HUFT Belonging, and the data backbone HUFT TryOn (launching in a few months) will need.
